Find an institution and a course

You need to be enrolled in an institution and in a course that is registered on the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS) before you can apply for a student visa to study in Australia.

Academic requirements (including evidence of English language skills) needed to study in Australia will vary between institutions.

Search the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS) to find the course and institution thatâ€™s right for you. Visit the institution website for details about their enrolment process.

Study costs

The costs of studying in Australia depend on the institution and the level of study you choose.

As an international student, your tuition fees are payable before you study. You can use the Search tool to find course details, such as their tuition fees. There may be additional costs for your course, including course materials and access to institution facilities.

You will also need to consider living and accommodation expenses while you are studying.

Student Visa - Living Costs and Evidence of Funds

International students are required to demonstrate and/or declare that they have genuine access to sufficient funds to be granted a student visa. Applicants may have to demonstrate sufficient funds to cover these expenses for the first one or two years in Australia, depending on their assessment level.

Scholarships

There are many scholarships, grants and bursaries available to help support you financially with your studies in Australia. They are offered by the Australian Government, education institutions, and a number of other public and private organisations.

To find out which scholarships you might be eligible for, use the search tool to find contact details for scholarship providers.

  1. Type in your area of study or an institution in the Search bar
  2. In the Search results page, click on the Scholarships tab
  3. From the list of available scholarships, apply the filter to match your preferences

Scholarship conditions are set by the institution or organisation offering the scholarship and applications must be done directly through the scholarship provider.
