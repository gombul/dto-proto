Setting up as an Independent Contractor

Below is information regarding setting up as an independent contractor in Queensland within the transport industry. Start a new search

Independent Contractors run their own business. Typically, they are engaged for a specific task or time and they negotiate their own fees and working arrangements. They can work for more than one client at a time and have a high level of control over how their work is done, including the choice to hire others to assist. Also called sub-contractors (subbies) or contractors, they generally pay their own super, tax and GST, use their own tools and equipment, bear responsibility for making a profit or loss, bear responsibility for poor work or injury and don't receive paid annual leave.

Understand your rights

Independent contractors have limited workplace rights. They are not entitled to conditions such as annual leave, sick leave and redundancy entitlements, unless they negotiate for these in their contract.

Find out more:

  • Visit the Fairwork Ombudsman website for information on sham contracting arrangements and how the Ombudsman can help you.
  • Contact WorkCover Queensland for information about work health, safety and workers’ compensation.
  • Use the Employee/contractor decision tool to find out whether you’re an employee or contractor for tax and super purposes.
  • Use the Independent contractors decision tool to find out what the courts consider when deciding whether someone is in an employee to employer relationship, or in an independent contractor to hirer relationship.
